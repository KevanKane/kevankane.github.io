TougeVenture
Game Project Design Develop
After numerous of unfinished game projects, I've made the decision to finally focus on developing a game that
I intend to market. I've come up with the concept of an endless driving game inspired by 90s Japanese street racing.

Why did I come up with that idea, you might ask?

Well, from what I've observed in the mobile games market, there's been a lack of racing games that are both entertaining
and visually appealing lately. As a former mobile gamer who largely enjoys arcade racing games, I've longed for a
high-quality 90s Japanese street racing experience on mobile. I've noticed others with similar interests expressing the
same desire. Thus, in mid-2021, I decided to create TougeVenture with my self-taught game development skills.
The first take of TougeVenture didn't quite live up to my dreams. horrible cars topologies, janky physics,
and there were minimal optimizations in the code. This was because at the time, I was still quite new to
game development. However, one aspect I'm proud of is the UI/UX and its graphic designs.
I decided to abandon the project and moved on to a new one, the game project didn't turn out to be as good
as I had in mind, but I did learn a lot of things to help me improve and become a better game developer.

After two years had passed without releasing a single game, I made the decision to get back on TougeVenture
in mid-2023. Upon returning to the project, I quickly realized that many aspects were amiss – the code, the models,
the interfaces, everything needed improvement. Therefore, I decided to reworked the whole game from the beginning.
I began by overhauling the car models, selecting a new lineup of cars and collecting new references. I adopted a
different approach to modeling the cars, applying a new art style, improving topologies, and more consistent models.
In contrast to the first TougeVenture, I chose for smoother car models. I also employed a different approach to
shading the cars using UV Maps, resulting in crisper detail fidelity compared to the typical 2160*2160 baked maps.
This comparison alone illustrates how much I've improved in 3D modeling, not to mention my advancement in other game development skills.
The cars documented here are among the early ones I've created for the game; a lot more will be added over time.
Please note that everything documented here is subject to change and may not represent the final product of the game.
