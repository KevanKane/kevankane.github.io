Game College Visual Design
This game was created as a final project for a college course, where the only requirement was to connect it to the course in some way. As a game developer, I embraced the challenge fully, turning the subject itself into the foundation of a game. Even as an art student studying the principles of art, I thought making a game would be a blast, something that would truly set my work apart from the rest of the class. The name translates to 'One More Assistance,' referencing the common scenario of art students seeking their professor's guidance before submitting their work for grading, often asking for approval on whether their piece is worthy. It also plays on an inside joke from my experience in the course, where I had to repeatedly redo artworks due to the professor's refusals. Since it revolved around art and its principles, I decided to create a puzzle game inspired by the course's subjects. I focused on four of the most memorable and crucial principles: Points and Lines, Shapes and Spaces, Colors and Values, and the Rhythm, Unity, and Harmony of art. Dividing it into 4 playable chapters. Points & Lines, Shapes & Space, Colors & Values, and Rhythm, Unity, and Harmony.
Aiming for a relaxing yet curiosity-piquing gameplay experience, I opted for a minimalistic style with calming colors. I chose a palette of green, blue, beige, and red to create a warm, inviting atmosphere almost like a comforting embrace. Additionally, the use of serif fonts lends the game a more refined and timeless aesthetic, enhancing its artistic feel.
Beyond the 25 individually crafted puzzles I designed, I also created the logo and illustrated a "Passepartout" for the game's icon and menu splash art. A nod to the way we physically submitted our artwork in the course. An example of both designs in use can be seen in the game's Main Menu.

Since I aimed for a minimalistic and calming gameplay experience, I experimented with sound design by using a simple click recorded from a mechanical keyboard and a typewriter’s clicking sound. As this game is a non-profit project, a true labor of love, I chose to incorporate soundtracks composed by Justin Karas to enhance the atmosphere.

Additionally, the game weaves in a subtle narrative, told from the perspective of a college student navigating the course, grappling with assignments, multiple revisions, and repeated refusals. I won't reveal too much, though, as I'd rather let players experience this small yet hopefully meaningful story for themselves. "Will you master the art of visual design, or will MCVD remain your greatest obstacle?" Another experience I poured into the game is that strange feeling of understanding everything, while at the same time, not quite. That's the sensation I want players to have. I want them to feel confident, thinking the puzzles are simple and obvious, only to realize they're more intricate than they seem. The challenge lies in observation, how the shapes form, how they unify, and how each element interacts with the others. Even though this game was developed in less than a week (about five days), I'm genuinely surprised by how it turned out. Some of my friends have played it and loved it, and I feel the difficulty strikes a nice balance, somewhere between easy and challenging. The game is now available for free for anyone to play, and I hope you enjoy the 15-minute (or more) experience, inspired by my journey through the course.
