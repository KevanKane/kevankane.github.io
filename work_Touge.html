TougeVenture
2023
Game Independent Project In Halt
Born from my passion for Japanese car culture and series like Wangan Midnight and Initial D, this project set out to bring an authentic Japanese street racing experience to mobile. As a longtime fan of the scene, it felt like the perfect game to create, at first...
The First Iteration As a solo developer, this was an incredibly ambitious project to undertake. I've been working on it since 2021, investing years into bringing the idea to life. Although the first outcome wasn't as polished as I had hoped, the project became a major milestone in my growth, teaching me invaluable lessons in game development. Looking back, the first iteration had its fair share of issues: janky physics, poor optimization, and messy modeling topology. The project itself has unfortunately been lost over time, largely due to my inexperience with project management. It wasn't my finest work, but as one of my earliest serious game development projects, it laid the foundation for the skills and workflow I have today. These are the only footages I can found on TougeVenture's First Iteration. I put the project on hold somewhere in 2022 after recognizing that its technical foundation would require a complete overhaul. Although I didn't yet have the expertise to tackle that challenge, the experience taught me valuable lessons that have influenced every project I've worked on since. The Second Iteration After shelving the first iteration in 2022, I spent time working on several smaller projects to improve my skills. By 2023, I felt ready to revisit the idea with a fresh perspective. This time, I reimagined it as a live-service oriented mobile game. Rather than following the common pay-to-win approach, my goal was to create a progression system that rewarded players through engaging, long-term gameplay. I wanted upgrading and customizing cars to feel satisfying, giving players meaningful goals without relying on unfair monetization.
I completely rebuilt the game from the ground up, this time using a lighter rendering pipeline to improve performance and scalability. Switching to an entirely new rendering pipeline was intimidating at first, but the process pushed me to learn and adapt. The experience proved invaluable, and today every project I develop uses this newer pipeline.
The difference between the first and second iterations is like night and day. I improved nearly every aspect of the game's development. From the programming and the game engine itself, Illustrations and into the UI/UX, every element became more refined, cohesive, and visually consistent.
During the time between iterations, I expanded my Blender and 3D modeling expertise, learning industry-standard techniques such as clean topology, efficient UV unwrapping, and texture baking. And that effort bore fruit in the form of cleaner, more detailed, and higher-quality assets. One of the biggest improvements gameplay-wise over the first iteration was the vehicle physics. I replaced the original system with a custom physics controller built from scratch using a raycast suspension approach. The difference was immediately noticeable, delivering a far more stable, responsive, and realistic driving experience.
Custom photo mode in-game
Why's the project In Halt? This project is currently in halt not because I lost interest, but because of its sheer scope. As a solo developer, I managed to build every aspect of the game myself, but doing so took nearly three years. I realized that continuing to pursue such an ambitious project alone wasn't the best use of my time. Instead, I chose to focus on smaller, more achievable projects that would allow me to grow as a developer, build a stronger portfolio, and establish a solid foundation. My goal is to eventually return to this project with a dedicated team, making it possible to realize the vision without spending a lifetime developing it alone. Another factor was the state of the racing game genre. I felt that racing games offered limited room for innovation while appealing to a relatively niche audience. As a live-service title, the project would also require long-term content updates, ongoing maintenance, and a committed player community to succeed. Given those challenges, I decided it was more practical to pause development rather than continue without a sustainable plan. Even so, this project remains deeply important to me, or even yet the most important. It taught me some of the most valuable lessons of my development journey, and I fully intend to return to it when the time, resources, and team are right.
